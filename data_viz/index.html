Complaints from Navient customers continue across US despite lawsuits, data shows

By Kiersten Hacker April 19, 2022


Student loan borrowers continued to file complaints against interacting with the student loan service, Navient, even after the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau filed a class action lawsuit against the company in 2017.

Since the CFPB filed the lawsuit in January 2017, consumers across the nation still filed complaints with the company for various issues, some related to the charges. According to data available from the CFPB Consumer Complaint Database, in 2017 there were 2,161 complaints filed against Navient in the U.S. from April 24 to Dec. 31.

Navient claimed that the lawsuit was unsubstantiated and in May 2020, the company filed a motion for it to be dismissed. However, this case is still open and in 2022 there have been 269 complaints filed against Navient from January to April.

In Maryland, borrowers sent 58 complaints to Navient in 2017 while borrowers from a western U.S. state of similar population size, like Colorado, sent 53 complaints. Customer experience with the company received complaints regardless of location, while the company argued the case should be dropped. Though total complaints in the U.S. declined from 2017 to 2022 for the months recorded, borrowers are still facing issues dealing the loan service.

State attorneys general pursued lawsuits after the CFPB, leading to 39 attorneys general joining a settlement in January, 2022. The settlement requires Navient to pay $1.85 billion in relief to private and federal student loan borrowers who suffered the consequences of the actions the company was charged with. After the settlement decision in January, Navient received 29 fewer complaints in February from borrowers nationwide.
